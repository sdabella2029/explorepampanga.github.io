Visit These Places

Featured Photos

Mount Arayat

An extinct volcano that stands majestically in the plains of Pampanga. The mountain offers hiking trails with breathtaking views of Central Luzon.

Giant Lantern Festival

Every December, San Fernando becomes the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines" with its spectacular Giant Lantern Festival featuring massive, intricately designed parols.

Famous Kapampangan Dishes

Pampanga is renowned as the Culinary Capital of the Philippines, offering delicious dishes like sisig, bringhe, and morcon that reflect its rich culinary heritage.

Pampanga Photo Collection

San Guillermo Parish Church

Bacolor, Pampanga

Known as the "Sunken Church," this historical site was partially buried by lahar during the 1991 Mt. Pinatubo eruption.

Clark Freeport Zone

Angeles City, Pampanga

A former US airbase transformed into a bustling economic zone with hotels, casinos, restaurants, and the Clark International Airport.

Aqua Planet Water Park

Clark Freeport Zone

The largest water park in the Philippines featuring exciting water slides, wave pools, and aquatic attractions for families.

St. James the Apostle Church

Guagua, Pampanga

Known as the "Sistine Chapel of the Philippines," this historic church features magnificent wood carvings and paintings.

Pampanga River

Across Pampanga

The second largest river on Luzon island, flowing through the heart of Pampanga and supporting agriculture and fishing.

Nayong Pilipino

Clark Freeport Zone

A cultural theme park showcasing replicas of famous Philippine landmarks and traditional houses from different regions.

Photography Tips

Best Time for Photos

Early morning (6-8 AM) and late afternoon (4-6 PM) provide the best lighting for capturing Pampanga's beautiful landscapes and architecture.

Festival Photography

During festivals, use faster shutter speeds to capture moving performances and vibrant colors. A tripod helps for night shots.

Food Photography

Pampanga's cuisine is visually appealing. Use natural light when possible and focus on the textures and colors of the dishes.
