Welcome to Pampanga

Pampanga is a province in the Central Luzon region of the Philippines. It is known as the "Culinary Capital of the Philippines" because of its rich food culture and delicious dishes that have gained recognition nationwide.

The province has a rich history dating back to pre-colonial times. It was one of the first eight provinces to revolt against Spanish colonization, and its people have maintained a strong cultural identity throughout centuries of change.

Located north of Manila, Pampanga is easily accessible and serves as a gateway to Northern Luzon. The province is home to Clark Freeport Zone, a former US airbase that has been transformed into a bustling economic hub with international airport, hotels, and entertainment facilities.

Cultural Highlights

Giant Lantern Festival

Every December, San Fernando City hosts the spectacular Giant Lantern Festival, where massive, intricately designed parols (lanterns) compete in a dazzling display of lights and creativity.

Historical Churches

Pampanga is home to centuries-old churches like the San Guillermo Parish Church in Bacolor and Betis Church in Guagua, showcasing Spanish colonial architecture and religious heritage.

Culinary Heritage

Kapampangan cuisine is renowned for its complexity and flavor. Famous dishes include sisig, bringhe, morcon, and various traditional sweets that reflect the province's rich culinary tradition.

Geography & Economy

Geographical Features

Pampanga is characterized by flat plains with Mount Arayat as its most prominent geographical feature. The Pampanga River, the second largest river on Luzon, flows through the province and supports agriculture in the region.

Agricultural Products

Pampanga is a major agricultural producer of rice, sugar cane, corn, and various fruits and vegetables. The province is also known for its fishing industry and aquaculture.

Economic Zones

Clark Freeport Zone and Angeles City serve as major economic drivers with international businesses, tourism facilities, and the Clark International Airport connecting the province to global markets.
